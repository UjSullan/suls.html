1. Exploring the Internal Profile of Batwan

Batwan (Garcinia binucao) is a sour fruit native to the Philippines, particularly in the Visayas region. It is green and round, with a tart flavor that is prized for its use as a natural souring agent in many traditional Filipino dishes like Kansi and Sinigang. The fruit is rarely eaten raw due to its intense sourness.

2. Dietary Benefits and Nutritional Values

Batwan is a good source of vitamin C, aiding in boosting the immune system and collagen production. It also contains antioxidants and has low calorie content, making it beneficial for digestion and metabolism. Its organic acids contribute to digestive health and stimulate appetite.

3. Cultural Significance

In Iloilo and Negros, Batwan is a culinary staple, essential in giving a distinct sourness to local dishes. Unlike tamarind, which is widely used in other regions, Batwan holds a special place in Visayan cooking and represents a regional identity that many Ilonggos are proud of.

4. Economic Contributions

Batwan trees are cultivated mainly for personal use and local markets. Small farmers sell fresh batwan fruits or use them to create processed products like dried batwan or batwan paste, creating opportunities for local food businesses and entrepreneurs specializing in native ingredients.

5. Sustainability and Conservation

Because Batwan grows naturally and with minimal intervention, it contributes to sustainable agriculture practices. Preserving and encouraging the planting of Batwan trees helps maintain local biodiversity and reduces dependence on imported souring agents, supporting traditional ecological knowledge and culinary heritage.
