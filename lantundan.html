1. Exploring the Internal Profile of Lantundan Banana

The Lantundan banana, also known as apple banana, is one of the most popular banana varieties in the Philippines. It is smaller than the typical Cavendish banana and has a slightly tangy, apple-like flavor. Its skin turns yellow with brown spots when ripe, while the flesh is sweet and creamy.

2. Dietary Benefits and Nutritional Values

Lantundan bananas are a great source of potassium, vitamin C, dietary fiber, and natural sugars, providing a quick source of energy. They also aid in digestion, help regulate blood pressure, and contribute to heart health due to their rich potassium content.

3. Cultural Significance

Bananas like the Lantundan are a staple fruit in Filipino households. They are often offered during fiestas and family gatherings and are used in traditional desserts like "minatamis na saging" and banana cue. The banana plant itself is also considered a symbol of resilience in Filipino culture.

4. Economic Contributions

Lantundan bananas are cultivated by small to medium-scale farmers across the Philippines. They are sold in wet markets, supermarkets, and even exported in small quantities, contributing to the livelihoods of many rural communities.
